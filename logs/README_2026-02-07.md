## Task 1 — Sanity-Check Baseline Neural Network Accuracy

### Summary
The baseline CNN achieved ~99.7% accuracy on the test set, which required careful interpretation to determine whether this performance reflects true generalization or optimistic evaluation.

### Findings
- The dataset was split using a **random window-level train/test split**.
- Windows are 5 seconds long with 50% overlap, meaning adjacent windows from the same trip are highly correlated.
- As a result, windows originating from the same physical trip or segment likely appear in both the training and test sets.
- This leads to **information leakage at the trip level**, making the classification task easier and inflating reported accuracy.

### Conclusion
- The baseline accuracy is **valid as a milestone** but represents an **optimistic upper bound** on performance.
- True real-world generalization requires evaluating the model on **entirely unseen trips**, not just unseen windows.
- A segment-level or trip-level split will be implemented in future evaluation to better approximate deployment conditions.

## Task 2 — Feature Importance Interpretation and Lock-In

### Summary
Feature visualizations from windowed sensor data were analyzed to determine which signals most strongly differentiate transportation modes.

### Feature Ranking

#### Tier 1 — Strongest Discriminators
- Accelerometer standard deviation:
  - `ax_corr`, `ay_corr`, `az_corr`
- Gyroscope standard deviation:
  - `gx`, `gy`, `gz`

**Rationale:**
- Walking exhibits significantly higher variance across all accelerometer and gyroscope axes due to periodic human motion and frequent orientation changes.
- Vehicle-based modes cluster tightly, showing lower inertial variance.

#### Tier 2 — Contextual Discriminator
- Barometric pressure standard deviation

**Rationale:**
- Pressure variance provides contextual information, particularly for distinguishing rail-based transportation (train, subway) from road-based modes (car, bus).

#### Expected Ambiguity
- Partial overlap between **car** and **bus** was observed across inertial features.
- This reflects similar operating conditions and is consistent with real-world expectations, not a modeling flaw.

## Locked Interpretation Statement (Slide-Ready)

> Exploratory analysis of windowed sensor features revealed strong separability between transportation modes. Walking exhibited significantly higher variance in both accelerometer and gyroscope signals across all axes, reflecting periodic human motion and frequent orientation changes. In contrast, vehicle-based modes showed lower and more tightly clustered inertial variance. Barometric pressure variance further contributed contextual information, particularly for distinguishing rail-based transportation from road-based modes. While car and bus data showed partial overlap—consistent with their similar operating conditions—this ambiguity aligns with real-world expectations.
